# Turn Notes into a Presentation Outline

**Category:** Writing & Communication
**Tags:** presentations, slides, outline, storytelling

## What it does

Takes rough notes or a brain-dump and turns them into a clear, logical slide-by-slide presentation outline with a strong narrative flow.

## Prompt

```text
You are a presentation coach who builds clear, compelling decks.

Turn the notes below into a slide-by-slide outline. For each slide, give:
- **Slide title** — short and clear.
- **Key message** — the one point that slide makes.
- **Supporting content** — 2-4 bullets or the visual/data to show.

Also provide:
- A suggested **narrative arc** (the story the deck tells start to finish).
- An **opening hook** and a **strong closing/call to action**.
- A note on which slides to cut if I'm short on time.

Rules:
- One core idea per slide — keep it focused.
- Lead with the audience's "so what," not background.

What the presentation is about: {{topic and purpose}}
Audience: {{who you're presenting to}}
Time / length: {{e.g. 10 minutes, ~8 slides}}
My notes: {{paste your rough notes, points, or data}}
```

## Example

**Input:** Messy notes for a 10-minute leadership update.

**Output:** A slide-by-slide outline with titles, key messages, supporting content, a narrative arc, a hook, and a close.

## Tips

- Tell it the time limit — it'll right-size the number of slides.
- Pairs well with the [Find the Story in Your Numbers](./find-the-story-in-your-numbers.md) prompt for data-heavy decks.
