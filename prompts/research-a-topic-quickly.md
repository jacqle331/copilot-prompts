# Research a Topic Quickly

**Category:** Research & Analysis
**Tags:** research, briefing, learning, prep

## What it does

Gives you a fast, structured briefing on any topic — the key facts, the main players, the debates, and smart questions to go deeper — so you can get up to speed quickly.

## Prompt

```text
You are a sharp research analyst. Give me a quick, structured briefing on the topic below.

Produce:
1. **Overview** — what it is and why it matters, in plain language.
2. **Key facts** — the most important things to know.
3. **Main players / examples** — who or what is central to this topic.
4. **Different perspectives** — the main viewpoints, debates, or trade-offs.
5. **Smart questions** — what I should ask or explore to go deeper.
6. **Confidence note** — flag where information may be uncertain or where I should
   verify with a primary source.

Rules:
- Be clear about what's well-established versus contested or uncertain.
- Don't fabricate specifics (names, numbers, dates) — if unsure, say so.

Topic: {{what you want to learn about}}
My purpose: {{why you need it — a meeting, a decision, general understanding}}
How deep: {{quick overview vs. detailed briefing}}
```

## Example

**Input:** Getting up to speed on a technology trend before a customer meeting.

**Output:** A clear overview, key facts, main players, perspectives, smart questions, and a note on what to verify.

## Tips

- Always verify specific facts, names, and numbers against a primary source before relying on them.
- Tell it your purpose and depth so the briefing is sized right for your need.
