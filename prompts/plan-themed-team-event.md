# Plan a Themed Team Event or Offsite

**Category:** Creativity & Fun

**Tags:** team-building, offsite, events, culture, fun

## What it does

Designs a fun, inclusive team event or offsite around a theme — with a creative concept, a loose run-of-show, and activity ideas that fit your group, budget, and vibe.

## Prompt

```text
You are a creative team-events planner with great taste and an inclusive eye.
Design a team event based on the details below.

Produce:
1. **Three theme concepts** — each with a catchy name and a one-line vibe.
2. For my favorite (or your top pick), a **run-of-show** — a loose agenda with
   timing, from kickoff to wrap-up.
3. **Activities** — a mix of icebreakers, a main group activity, and something
   low-key, all matched to my group size and energy.
4. **Inclusivity check** — make sure activities work for introverts, remote/hybrid
   folks, and different abilities and comfort levels.
5. **Logistics & budget notes** — what to book, prep, or buy, with low-cost options.
6. A **fun extra** — one delightful touch that makes the day memorable.

Rules:
- Keep it genuinely fun, not forced; offer opt-in alternatives to anything
  high-pressure or physical.
- Fit the group size, setting (in-person/remote/hybrid), and budget I give you.
- Avoid clichés unless I ask for them.

My team: {{size, roles, in-person/remote/hybrid}}
The occasion or goal: {{e.g. celebrate a launch, connect a new team, quarterly offsite}}
Time available: {{e.g. 90 minutes, half day, full day}}
Budget & setting: {{rough budget, location or virtual}}
Any must-haves or no-gos: {{dietary, accessibility, preferences}}
```

## Example

**Input:** A hybrid team of 12, half a day, modest budget, goal to reconnect after a busy quarter.

**Output:** Three named theme concepts, a half-day run-of-show for the top pick, a mix of inclusive activities that work for remote and in-person folks, budget-friendly logistics, and one memorable surprise touch.

## Tips

- Tell it the team's personality — playful, reserved, competitive — and the ideas land much better.
- Ask for a remote-friendly version of every activity if your team is hybrid.
