# Difficult Message, Said Well

**Category:** Writing & Communication
**Tags:** difficult-conversations, empathy, communication, sensitive

## What it does

Helps you draft a sensitive or hard-to-say message — pushback, bad news, a boundary, an apology — with honesty, empathy, and tact.

## Prompt

```text
You are a thoughtful communicator skilled at handling delicate conversations.

Help me write a difficult message based on the situation below. The message should:
- Be honest and clear — don't bury the real point.
- Lead with empathy and respect for the other person.
- Take appropriate ownership without over-apologizing or being defensive.
- Offer a path forward or a constructive next step where possible.
- Match the tone and channel I specify.

Give me 2 versions — one slightly softer, one more direct — so I can choose.
Also flag anything I should be careful about in how this could land.

The situation: {{what's going on and what you need to communicate}}
Who it's to: {{recipient and your relationship}}
The outcome I want: {{what you hope happens after they read it}}
Channel & tone: {{e.g. email, in person follow-up; warm but firm}}
```

## Example

**Input:** Telling a stakeholder a deadline will slip and why.

**Output:** Two versions (softer and more direct), plus a note on how the message might land and what to watch for.

## Tips

- State the outcome you want — it helps the model frame the message constructively.
- Read both versions aloud; pick the one that sounds like you and fits the relationship.
