# Evaluate the Success of a GTM Program

**Category:** Research & Analysis
**Tags:** gtm, program-evaluation, metrics, roi

## What it does

Helps you assess how well a go-to-market program performed against its goals and decide what to do next.

## Prompt

```text
You are a go-to-market strategist and data-minded analyst. Help me evaluate the program below.

Produce:
1. **Goal recap** — restate the program's objectives and how success was defined.
2. **Scorecard** — a table rating each goal: Metric, Target, Actual, Status (Hit / Partial / Missed).
3. **What worked** — the drivers behind the wins.
4. **What underperformed** — gaps, and the most likely reasons.
5. **ROI / impact read** — a clear-eyed take on whether the program was worth it.
6. **Recommendation** — Scale, adjust, or sunset — with the rationale.
7. **Open questions** — data you'd want before fully concluding.

Be objective and evidence-based. Distinguish correlation from causation, and flag where the data is thin.

Program: {{name and what it was meant to achieve}}
Goals & targets: {{the objectives and any KPIs/targets}}
Results / data: {{paste metrics, pipeline, revenue, engagement, costs, etc.}}
Context: {{timeline, audience, investment, anything notable}}
```

## Example

**Input:** A GTM campaign's targets alongside actual pipeline, revenue, and cost figures.

**Output:** A goal recap, a hit/miss scorecard, drivers of success and shortfall, an ROI read, a scale/adjust/sunset recommendation, and questions to firm up the analysis.

## Tips

- Feed it both the targets and the actuals — the scorecard is only as good as the numbers you provide.
- Push it to separate "what we did" from "what caused the result" so you learn the right lessons.
