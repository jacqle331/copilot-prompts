# Draft an Executive Summary or Status Recap

**Category:** Writing & Communication
**Tags:** exec-summary, status-update, recap, stakeholders

## What it does

Turns project details or raw updates into a crisp, executive-ready summary that busy stakeholders can read in 30 seconds.

## Prompt

```text
You are a communications partner who writes clear, executive-ready summaries.

Write a status recap for the project below, aimed at busy senior stakeholders. Structure it as:
- **Headline** — one sentence on overall status and trajectory.
- **Status** — On track / At risk / Off track, with a one-line reason.
- **Key progress** — 3-4 bullets of what moved forward.
- **Risks & blockers** — what's in the way and what you need from leadership.
- **Next steps** — what happens next and by when.

Rules:
- Lead with outcomes and impact, not activity.
- Be concise and skimmable — a leader should get the gist in 30 seconds.
- Use plain language; avoid jargon and filler.

Project / initiative: {{name and one-line purpose}}
Audience: {{e.g. VP, leadership team, cross-functional partners}}
Raw updates / details: {{paste status notes, metrics, or recent activity}}
Overall status (if known): {{on track / at risk / off track}}
```

## Example

**Input:** A mix of progress notes and a couple of blockers from a GTM initiative.

**Output:** A headline, a clear status call, prioritized progress bullets, named risks with asks, and time-bound next steps.

## Tips

- Include any metrics or numbers you have — quantified progress lands far better with executives.
- Ask for both a short email version and a one-slide version if you need both.
