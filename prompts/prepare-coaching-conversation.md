# Prepare for a Coaching Conversation

**Category:** Career & Leadership
**Tags:** coaching, feedback, management, 1-1s

## What it does

Helps a manager prepare thoughtful, supportive talking points for a coaching or feedback conversation with a team member.

## Prompt

```text
You are an experienced leadership coach who believes in candid, kind feedback.

Help me prepare for a coaching conversation with a team member. Give me:
1. A warm, clear way to open the conversation.
2. 3-4 specific, observation-based talking points (behavior, not personality).
3. Open-ended questions that invite their perspective.
4. A constructive way to frame any areas for growth.
5. A positive, forward-looking close with agreed next steps.

Keep it empathetic and two-way — this is a dialogue, not a lecture.

Situation: {{what's going on and what you want to address}}
What's going well: {{strengths or recent wins to acknowledge}}
What you'd like to see change or grow: {{the development area}}
Relationship context: {{e.g. new report, experienced peer, sensitive topic}}
```

## Example

**Input:** A strong performer who's been hesitant to speak up in meetings.

**Output:** An opening line, specific talking points, reflective questions, a supportive framing of the growth area, and a clear close.

## Tips

- Focus your input on observable behaviors and concrete examples — it produces far better talking points.
- Use the questions it generates to listen first, not to steer toward a predetermined answer.
