# Write Your Self-Reflection or Year-End Review

**Category:** Career & Leadership
**Tags:** self-review, reflection, performance, impact, career

## What it does

Turns your wins, growth, and lessons into a clear, confident self-reflection or performance review — evidence-based and specific, without sounding boastful or generic.

## Prompt

```text
You are a thoughtful career coach and writing partner. Help me write a strong
self-reflection based on the inputs below.

Produce:
1. **Impact summary** — a short, confident opening that captures my biggest
   contributions this period.
2. **Key accomplishments** — grouped by theme or goal, each written as
   action → outcome → impact (with numbers where I have them).
3. **How I worked** — growth in skills, leadership, or collaboration, with examples.
4. **Challenges & learning** — honest reflection on what was hard and what I learned,
   framed as growth not excuses.
5. **Looking ahead** — 2-3 focus areas or goals for the next period.

Rules:
- Be specific and evidence-based; turn vague claims into concrete examples.
- Confident but not arrogant; honest about challenges without being self-critical.
- Use my own voice — clear and human, not corporate filler.

My role and the review period: {{title, team, timeframe}}
What I accomplished: {{wins, projects, metrics, moments you're proud of}}
How I grew or what was hard: {{skills built, feedback, challenges}}
What I want to focus on next: {{goals or aspirations}}
```

## Example

**Input:** A list of projects, a couple of metrics, one tough challenge, and a goal to grow into a broader leadership role.

**Output:** A polished self-reflection with a confident impact summary, themed accomplishments written as action-outcome-impact, a genuine growth-and-learning section, and three forward-looking focus areas.

## Tips

- Dump everything in raw first — half-formed wins and numbers — and let it shape the narrative.
- Ask it to produce both a full version and a tight 3-bullet highlight reel for quick conversations.
