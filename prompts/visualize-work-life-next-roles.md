# Visualize Your Work Life & Next-Step Roles

**Category:** Career & Leadership
**Tags:** career-growth, visualization, image-generation, reflection

## What it does

Generates a clean cartoon whiteboard-style image that maps your work life — what you do, who you work with, your role, your values — and surfaces aspirational next-step roles you could grow toward. Best paired with your resume for added depth.

## Prompt

```text
Create a photorealistic image in a clean cartoon whiteboard sketch style that
visualizes my work life. The image should clearly illustrate:

- **What I do** — my core responsibilities and the kind of work I spend time on
- **Who I work with** — my customers, teammates, partners, and stakeholders
- **My role** — my title and where I sit in the bigger picture
- **My values** — what I stand for and how I like to work
- **What's important to me** — what motivates me and what I care about most
- **My next-step roles** — 2-4 possible future roles I could aspire toward,
  shown as a clear path forward, based on my experience and the work I do

Design guidance:
- Clean cartoon whiteboard sketch aesthetic: hand-drawn lines, simple icons,
  labeled boxes and arrows, a few accent colors on a white background.
- Organized and easy to read, like a thoughtful career map drawn on a whiteboard.
- Make the "next-step roles" section visually prominent as a growth path.

About me: {{describe your current role, responsibilities, who you work with,
your values, and what matters to you}}

Resume: {{upload or paste your resume so the roles and path reflect your real
experience}}
```

## Example

**Input:** A short description of your role plus your uploaded resume.

**Output:** A whiteboard-style career map image showing your current work life and a visible path toward 2-4 aspirational next roles.

## Tips

- **Upload your resume** — it makes the suggested next-step roles far more accurate and personal.
- The more honest detail you give about your values and what energizes you, the more meaningful the map.
- Re-run it once a year, or after a big role change, to see how your path evolves.
- If your tool can't read uploads, paste your resume text directly into the `Resume` section.
