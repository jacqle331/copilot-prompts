# Build a Time-Blocked Daily Plan

**Category:** Productivity
**Tags:** time-blocking, focus, planning, calendar, deep-work

## What it does

Turns your priorities and meetings into a realistic, hour-by-hour plan for the day — protecting time for deep work and building in buffers so the day actually holds up.

## Prompt

```text
You are a pragmatic productivity coach. Build me a time-blocked plan for today.

Using my priorities, fixed commitments, and energy patterns, produce:
1. A **time-blocked schedule** from start to end of day, in a simple table:
   Time, Block, Type (Deep work / Meeting / Admin / Break / Buffer).
2. **Top 3 outcomes** — the things that make today a win if nothing else gets done.
3. **Deep-work protection** — at least one uninterrupted focus block for my most
   important task, placed when my energy is highest.
4. **Buffers** — short gaps between meetings and a catch-up block for overflow.
5. A **realism check** — if I've scheduled more than the day can hold, tell me
   what to cut, defer, or shorten.

Rules:
- Respect my fixed meetings exactly; build everything else around them.
- Don't pack the day wall-to-wall — protect breaks and transition time.
- Match heavy thinking tasks to my high-energy window, light tasks to low-energy times.

My priorities today: {{your must-dos and goals}}
Fixed commitments: {{meetings/appointments with times}}
My working hours: {{e.g. 8:30am–5pm}}
When I focus best: {{e.g. mornings, after lunch}}
```

## Example

**Input:** Three priorities, two fixed meetings, a 9–5 day, "I focus best in the morning."

**Output:** An hour-by-hour table with a protected morning deep-work block, meetings preserved, buffers between them, a top-3 outcomes list, and a note to defer one task because the day was overbooked.

## Tips

- Tell it your real energy pattern — the schedule gets far more usable.
- Re-run it midday if the morning blew up; it'll re-plan the afternoon around what's left.
