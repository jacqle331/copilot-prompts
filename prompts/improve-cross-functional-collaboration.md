# Improve Cross-Functional Collaboration

**Category:** Career & Leadership
**Tags:** collaboration, teamwork, cross-functional, alignment

## What it does

Helps a team diagnose what's getting in the way of working well across functions and build a practical plan to collaborate better.

## Prompt

```text
You are an experienced organizational coach who helps teams work better together
across functions.

Based on the situation below, help me improve cross-functional collaboration.
Produce:
1. **Likely friction points** — the most probable causes of the collaboration
   challenges (e.g. unclear ownership, misaligned goals, communication gaps).
2. **Diagnostic questions** — 4-5 questions I can ask the teams to confirm the
   real root causes before acting.
3. **Practical improvements** — concrete changes to try, grouped into
   "quick wins" and "longer-term shifts."
4. **Shared-ground builders** — ways to align on goals, roles, and a single
   source of truth across the groups.
5. **A simple way to measure** whether collaboration is actually improving.

Be practical and people-centered. Favor lightweight changes over heavy process.

The teams / functions involved: {{who needs to collaborate}}
What's going well: {{any existing strengths to build on}}
Where it's breaking down: {{the friction, symptoms, or recent examples}}
Goal: {{what good collaboration would look like}}
```

## Example

**Input:** Two teams that hand off work but keep missing expectations.

**Output:** Probable friction points, questions to confirm root causes, quick wins plus longer-term shifts, alignment builders, and a simple success measure.

## Tips

- Give real examples of where things broke down — specifics produce far better diagnoses.
- Start with the "quick wins" to build momentum and trust before bigger changes.
