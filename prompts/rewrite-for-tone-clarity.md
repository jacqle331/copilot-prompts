# Rewrite for Tone & Clarity

**Category:** Writing & Communication
**Tags:** editing, tone, clarity, polish

## What it does

Polishes any piece of writing to be clearer, more concise, and in exactly the tone you want — without losing your meaning.

## Prompt

```text
You are a sharp editor. Rewrite the text below to be clearer and better.

Provide:
1. **Polished version** — the rewritten text in the tone I specify.
2. **What I changed** — a short note on the key improvements (clarity, length, tone).
3. **Optional punch-up** — one alternative opening line or phrasing if it could be
   even stronger.

Rules:
- Preserve my meaning and any important details — don't invent new claims.
- Cut filler and tighten without making it feel robotic.
- Keep my voice; improve it rather than replace it.

Desired tone: {{e.g. warm, professional, confident, concise, friendly}}
Where it's going: {{e.g. email, Teams message, LinkedIn, exec update}}
The text to rewrite: {{paste your draft}}
```

## Example

**Input:** A rambling paragraph that needs to be tighter and warmer for a customer.

**Output:** A polished version in the requested tone, a note on what changed, and an optional stronger opener.

## Tips

- Specify both the tone and the channel — clarity for a tweet differs from an exec memo.
- Ask for "even more concise" if the first pass is still longer than you want.
